Data Visualisation Tool - Roadmap Priorities

Goal
Build the strongest possible product around transparent, upload-first analytics for structured files.

Priority Framework
Each item is scored on:
- product value
- differentiation value
- implementation leverage

Top Priority Themes
- make explanations clearer
- improve trust and validation
- strengthen chart guidance
- deepen workflow value after upload


Tier 1 - Highest Priority

1. Domain rule packs
Score: 10/10
Why it matters
This is the strongest way to increase product intelligence without AI and create a real moat.
Examples
- crime tables
- health tables
- education tables
- finance and budget tables
- census and population tables
Outcome
Better metric selection, better summaries, better warnings, and better recommendations within familiar data domains.

2. Stronger consistency checks
Score: 9.5/10
Why it matters
Trust is one of the product’s biggest advantages. Strong validation reinforces that.
Examples
- total row validation
- percentage consistency checks
- duplicate summary detection
- summary-vs-detail mismatch warnings
- suspicious rate/count combinations
Outcome
Users trust the tool more because it does not just explain the data; it also checks whether the dataset behaves as expected.

3. Better chart recommendation scoring
Score: 9/10
Why it matters
Chart choice is one of the most important beginner pain points.
Examples
- stronger top-N triggers
- automatic rejection of weak pie charts
- improved timeline chart selection
- horizontal bars for long labels
- heatmap detection for row-column-value layouts
Outcome
The tool feels much smarter and safer during the first charting experience.

4. More human explanation quality
Score: 9/10
Why it matters
The app is already smart enough to say useful things. The next step is saying them more naturally and with less noise.
Examples
- shorter summaries
- fewer repetitive cautions
- better phrasing for panel datasets
- volume-aware wording
- stronger “what matters most” emphasis
Outcome
The product feels more polished and more trustworthy.


Tier 2 - Strong Product Expansion

5. Explainable recommendation details
Score: 8.5/10
Why it matters
This makes the “transparent intelligence” promise even stronger.
Examples
- why this field was chosen
- why this chart was recommended
- what rule triggered a warning
- what aggregation was applied
Outcome
A major differentiator versus AI tools and opaque BI helpers.

6. Better report outputs
Score: 8.5/10
Why it matters
Reports are one of the easiest ways to turn the tool into something shareable and useful beyond the app.
Examples
- executive summary section
- chart explanations in exported reports
- cleaner ranking sections
- domain-aware report language
Outcome
Users can share findings without rewriting everything manually.

7. Panel-dataset intelligence
Score: 8/10
Why it matters
Many public and operational datasets are country-year, state-year, or category-time panels.
Examples
- latest-year snapshot mode
- growth over time summaries
- strongest risers and decliners
- stable vs volatile groups
Outcome
The tool becomes especially strong on real-world longitudinal datasets.

8. Smarter data quality center
Score: 8/10
Why it matters
The quality center should feel like guidance, not a wall of warnings.
Examples
- grouped caution summaries
- better missingness explanation
- suspicious-field highlights
- field-priority scoring
Outcome
Better usability and less noise.


Tier 3 - Workflow Strengthening

9. Saved analysis templates
Score: 7.5/10
Why it matters
Users often repeat the same analysis patterns across similar files.
Outcome
Faster repeat usage and better workflow retention.

10. More chart types
Score: 7.5/10
Why it matters
Useful, but only after chart recommendation quality is strong enough to guide them well.
Examples
- stacked bar
- grouped bar
- area comparison
- density plot
- correlation heatmap

11. Better dashboard composition
Score: 7/10
Why it matters
Improves presentation and comparison workflows.
Examples
- drag-to-reorder
- chart resizing
- cleaner grid presets
- dashboard annotations

12. Performance improvements for larger files
Score: 7/10
Why it matters
Important as real usage grows.
Examples
- cached profiling
- smarter sampling
- incremental preview rendering
- faster repeated transformations


Suggested Build Order

Phase 1
- domain rule packs
- stronger consistency checks
- better chart recommendation scoring

Phase 2
- more human explanation quality
- explainable recommendation details
- better report outputs

Phase 3
- panel-dataset intelligence
- smarter data quality center
- saved analysis templates

Phase 4
- more chart types
- dashboard composition improvements
- performance optimization


Most Important Strategic Principle
Do not try to become a weaker version of AI analytics tools.
Become the strongest possible transparent, deterministic analytics layer for uploaded structured files.


Definition of Success
The product should make a user say:

"I uploaded a file, immediately understood what it contained, got useful chart guidance, and trusted the reasoning because it was clear and repeatable."
